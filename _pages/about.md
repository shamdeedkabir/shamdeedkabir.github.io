I am currently a second-year Ph.D. student in Bioinformatics Data Science at the University of Delaware at the [Abasht Lab](https://sites.udel.edu/abashtlab/). I graduated _summa cum laude_ from Colorado College with a B.A. in Computer Science. Before starting my Ph.D., I worked with [Dr. Sara Hanson](https://www.coloradocollege.edu/basics/contact/directory/people/hanson_sara_jeanette.html) on centromere genetics and [Dr. Csaba Kerepesi](https://sztaki.hun-ren.hu/en/organisation/departments/ilab/csaba-kerepesi) on epigenetic aging. I also worked as a programming analyst intern at [Lexidyne, LLC](https://lexidyne.com/).

My research involves statistical genetics and computational biology approaches to address the genome, transcriptome, and metabolome to investigate fundamental genetics and diseases. My doctoral research is focused on spatio-temporally resolved genomic and epigenomic regulatory mechanisms of embryogenesis and myogenesis. Additionally, I am investigating the genetic basis of myopathies, growth, and feed efficiency traits in commercial broiler chickens.
